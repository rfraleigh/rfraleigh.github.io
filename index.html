I help people navigate the data landscape surrounding
complex people problems.

As an Assistant Research Professor at Penn State, I build visual analytics that contextualize information into strategic options so that decision-makers can make informed decisions.

My current research lies at the intersection of complex biological systems, global health security, and psychology.

I work with the Applied Research Lab - Department of Data Sciences and the Applied Biological Research Lab

My Why: Equipping people with the tools and insight to navigate forward.

The stakes can be high and choices have impact. I believe in maintaining a high level of transparency in the data exploration process so that decisions can be weighed relative to the context of data landscape and project goals. I bring a deep level of empathy to the decision-making process.
